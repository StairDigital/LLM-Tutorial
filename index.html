The one skill that matters most

The four-part prompt formula

The gap between a useless answer and a brilliant one is almost never the tool. It is the prompt. Build every important instruction from these four parts.

Role
Who it should be
"Act as an experienced property consultant…"
Task
What you want done
"…write a listing description…"
Context
The facts & the reader
"…a 2BHK near the metro, for a young family…"
Format
How it should look
"…90 words, warm tone, end with a question."

Role + Task + Context + Format = a precise, reliable answer.

How to use ChatGPT

The assistant the masterclass demonstrates in most detail. You type an instruction, it replies, you refine. Here is the screen and the first five minutes.

  1. Open it. Go to chatgpt.com or install the app. Create a free account.
  2. Find the message box at the bottom. Everything starts there.
  3. Send your first prompt using the four-part formula. Press enter.
  4. Refine in the same box. Ask for it shorter, warmer, or with a price added.
  5. Attach a document with the plus button to summarise a brochure or contract.

Put it to work

For a lawyer, and for everyone else

The same tools, pointed at real tasks. Lawyers get detailed workflows with the cautions that matter; everyone else gets the everyday wins.

For everyone Everyday wins

  • Draft and tidy emails, messages and applications
  • Explain anything confusing, from a bill to a contract, in plain words
  • Plan a trip, a week, or a study schedule
  • Summarise long articles, reports or videos into the key points
  • Compare two options, like insurance policies, in a table
  • Learn a new subject at exactly your level

For lawyers Strong, lower-risk uses

  • Summarise long judgments and contracts into plain English
  • Draft routine correspondence and internal notes
  • Build a dated chronology from a bundle of documents
  • Compare two drafts clause by clause
  • Explain a difficult position to a client simply
  • Stress-test arguments by asking for the other side's case

Lawyer workflows

⚠ The non-negotiable cautions for legal work

  • Verify every citation. AI can invent cases, statutes and quotes that look real. Check every authority against the primary source.
  • Protect confidentiality. Do not paste privileged or client-identifying material into a consumer tool without checking your policy and the tool's data settings.
  • It is not legal advice. Every output is a drafting aid. Judgement, and the duty to client and court, stay with you.
  • Mind the jurisdiction. Court and bar guidance on AI is still developing. Keep a human firmly in the loop.
